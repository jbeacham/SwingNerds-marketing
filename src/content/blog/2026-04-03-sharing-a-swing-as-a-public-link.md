Sometimes you just want to send someone a swing. A buddy who is going to roast you. A coach who is going to look at it later. A spouse who has been wondering what you do in there for two hours. The share feature in SwingNerds exists for that.

## What it does

You pick a swing in your account, hit the Share button, and you get a public link. Anyone with the link can open it in a browser and watch the swing. They do not need a SwingNerds account. They do not need to download anything.

What they see is a clean playback of the clip with the shot data burned onto the frame: carry, ball speed, club path, whatever metrics you want shown. The video is the same one in your account. The data is the same data the launch monitor recorded.

You can pick which numbers appear on the share. The defaults work for most cases. If the swing is your worst hosel rocket of the year and the only number you want to show is the carry distance because it was somehow good, you can do that.

## Why a public link, not an account-only feature

Private sharing inside an app is fine for the people who are already in the app. The reason a public link matters is that 99% of the people you want to send a swing to are not in the app. They are in their text messages, on Reddit, on Twitter, on Discord, in a group chat, in an email to their coach.

A public link works in all of those places. You paste it, the recipient clicks it, the swing plays. No friction.

## What is on the page

The share page is intentionally minimal:

- The video, autoplaying, on a clean background
- The shot's selected metrics, rendered as overlays on the frame
- A small "via SwingNerds" mark at the corner
- The player's name (if you have set one in your account, otherwise omitted)

That is it. No comment section. No account prompts. No popups. The goal of the page is to play the swing and get out of the way.

## What gets burned into the frame

When you create a share, the metrics you pick are rendered into the video itself. They are part of the visual frame, not an overlay floating above. That means:

- The video looks the same when downloaded as it does when played in a browser.
- Screenshots and screen recordings of the page show the metrics correctly.
- Reposting on platforms like Twitter or Instagram preserves the metrics, since they are baked into the video.

You can choose any subset of:

- Carry distance
- Total distance
- Ball speed
- Club head speed
- Smash factor
- Spin rate, spin axis
- Launch angle, launch direction
- Attack angle
- Club path, face angle, face-to-path
- Apex height
- Side carry

Pick three or four for clarity. More than five tends to clutter the frame.

## Public, but not findable

A swing share is public in the sense that anyone with the link can view it. It is not public in the sense of being indexed in search engines or showing up in any directory. The links are unguessable strings. If you give someone a link, they have it. If you do not, they do not.

You can also delete a share at any time. The original swing in your account is untouched; just the public page goes away.

## What this looks like for facilities and dealers

Two of our audience tiers (commercial facilities and dealers) get an extra wrinkle on shares: the customer's facility or dealer logo appears on the share page alongside the swing. So when a player at a branded sim facility posts their swing on social, the facility's logo is on the video too.

This was not designed as a marketing channel originally. It became one. People share their best swings from a branded bay, the bay's logo travels with the video, and that bay gets a small ambient promotional benefit out of every share.

For individual golfers, no logo appears (other than the small "via SwingNerds" mark). The video is yours, on a clean page.

## Why it matters

Most swing review tools assume you are reviewing alone, in front of your own data. That is true a lot of the time. But a meaningful chunk of why people record swings in the first place is to talk about them with someone else. A coach. A friend. A community.

The share feature is the bridge. It takes a swing that lives in your account and turns it into a thing you can hand to anyone, in any conversation, in three seconds.
