The standard swing is one of those features that sounds small until you start using it. Here is what it is and why it ends up being one of the most-used parts of the product.

## What it is

For each club in your bag, you can mark one of your saved swings as your **standard**. From then on, every new swing with that club has a built-in comparison target.

Hit a 7-iron. Pull up the swing. The "compare against standard" button is right there. Two clips, side by side, your swing and your benchmark. No browsing, no searching, no remembering which old swing was your good one.

That is the entire feature.

## Why this is more useful than it sounds

Without a standard, every comparison requires a decision. "Which of my old swings should I compare this to?" That decision is small but constant, and small constant decisions are the kind that quietly stop you from using a feature.

With a standard, the decision is gone. The comparison is the default. You hit a swing, you compare it. Over a few weeks, this changes how you practice. Not because the feature itself is profound, but because the friction is zero.

## How to pick a standard

This is the part that bothers people the first time they encounter the feature. "Which swing should be my standard? Should it be my best ever? Should it be my most typical? Should I update it?"

There is no one right answer, but here are the patterns that work for most people:

### Option 1: Your best recent swing

The simplest approach. Find a swing that felt good and produced a good result. Mark it as your standard. Update it occasionally as you have better swings.

This is what most people do. The standard becomes a moving target, slowly improving as you do.

### Option 2: A specific reference swing

Some players pick a specific swing for a specific reason. The swing right after a good lesson. The swing where they nailed a particular feel. The swing they want to try to repeat.

This works well if you are working on a specific change with a coach.

### Option 3: A pro reference swing

If you have a clip of a tour pro hitting the same club from a similar angle, you can set that as your standard. Every swing you hit gets compared against the pro's clip.

This is mostly useful for big-picture sequence comparisons. Tour pro swings are not directly imitable for most players, but the pattern of how things sequence is.

### Option 4: Update the standard every session

Some players treat the standard as a "best of the day" rolling reference. They update it whenever a new swing is clearly better than the current one. This keeps the comparison challenging.

Pick whichever fits how you practice. You can change the standard any time. There is no penalty for changing it often.

## Why per-club, not one swing for everything

Different clubs are different jobs. Comparing a 7-iron swing against a driver swing tells you almost nothing useful. Even comparing a 7-iron against a pitching wedge is mostly noise.

So standards are per club. Your driver standard, your 7-iron standard, your wedge standard, all separate. Each one is the right reference for that club specifically.

If you do not have a standard set for a given club yet, the comparison feature still works manually with any swing you pick.

## How it shows up in the app

A few places the standard quietly appears:

- **In shot detail.** Next to each new shot, the "compare against standard" button is one click.
- **In the swing library.** The standard for each club has a small star or badge so you can find it again easily.
- **In the iOS app.** Same flow. Hit a swing in the bay, open it on your phone, the standard is one tap away.
- **In summaries.** Some "how am I doing this session" views compare your session swings against your standards in aggregate.

You do not have to interact with the standard for it to be useful. It is just there, waiting, when you want to compare.

## What it does not do

- **It does not score your swing against the standard.** The comparison is visual and on-demand. You decide what is better and what is worse. The system shows you the two clips and the data; the judgment is yours.
- **It does not auto-update.** When you hit a swing that you like more than your current standard, you have to pick "set as standard." We have considered auto-updating but the explicit pick is more useful in practice.
- **It does not work across launch monitor brands automatically.** If you switch from a launch monitor that gave you a particular dataset to one that gives a different dataset, the older standards still exist as videos. The data side may not match perfectly across the switch.

## Why it matters

Practice is mostly about consistency, not about producing one great swing. The standard swing turns "how does this swing compare to a swing I trust" into a one-click question. Over hundreds of practice swings, that one click adds up.

It is the simplest feature on this list. It is also the one most people end up using the most.
