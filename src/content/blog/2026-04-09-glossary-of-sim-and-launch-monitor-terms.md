A reference page. Every term you are likely to run into around launch monitors, sim bays, and swing video. Use it however you would use a glossary: skim it, search it, or come back to it when something on a screen does not make sense.

The definitions are intentionally short. Each one tells you what the term means, not how to think about it.

## Ball flight metrics

**Ball speed.** How fast the ball is moving the moment it leaves the club face. Reported in mph or kph.

**Launch angle.** The vertical angle the ball leaves the face at, measured in degrees above level.

**Launch direction.** The horizontal angle the ball leaves the face at, measured in degrees left or right of the target line.

**Spin rate.** How fast the ball is rotating, measured in revolutions per minute (RPM).

**Spin axis.** The tilt of the ball's spin, measured in degrees. A ball spinning purely backward has a spin axis of zero. A tilted axis produces a draw or a fade.

**Apex.** The highest point of the ball's flight, measured in feet, yards, or meters.

**Carry distance.** How far the ball traveled in the air before first touching the ground.

**Total distance.** Carry plus rollout. Where the ball ended up.

**Side carry.** How far offline the ball was when it landed, measured to the left or right of the target line.

**Hang time.** How long the ball was in the air, measured in seconds.

**Descent angle.** The angle the ball was traveling at when it landed, measured in degrees below level.

## Club delivery metrics

**Club head speed.** How fast the head of the club is moving at impact.

**Smash factor.** Ball speed divided by club head speed. A measure of how much of the club's energy was transferred to the ball.

**Attack angle.** Whether the club was traveling down, level, or up at the moment of impact, measured in degrees.

**Club path.** The direction the head was traveling at impact, relative to the target line.

**Face angle.** Where the club face was pointed at impact, relative to the target line.

**Face-to-path.** The face angle minus the club path. A useful single number describing what the face was doing relative to the swing direction.

**Dynamic loft.** The actual loft of the face at impact, which can differ from the club's static loft because of how the player presents the club through the strike.

**Loft.** The angle of the face relative to vertical, built into the club itself.

**Lie angle.** The angle of the shaft relative to the ground when the club is at address.

## Launch monitor concepts

**Camera-based (photometric) launch monitor.** Uses one or more high-speed cameras to photograph the ball and club at impact and just after.

**Doppler radar launch monitor.** Uses radar signals to track the ball through its flight and works out launch and spin numbers from the bounced signal.

**Combined launch monitor.** Uses both cameras and radar, typically cameras for impact and radar for ball flight.

**Marked ball.** A golf ball with reflective dots or a logo positioned to help camera-based launch monitors detect spin more accurately.

**Stickers (or alignment dots).** Small reflective stickers some golfers add to their own balls so a camera-based launch monitor can read spin off a ball they prefer to play.

## Simulator concepts

**Sim bay.** A space set up for indoor golf, usually with a hitting mat, a launch monitor, an impact screen or a net, and a projector or TV.

**GSPro.** A widely used third-party simulator software that ingests data from many launch monitor brands and runs simulated rounds on virtual courses.

**Hitting mat.** The surface the ball is struck off, indoors. Mats vary in material, thickness, and how forgiving they are on the wrists.

**Impact screen.** A heavy fabric screen the ball is struck into. The projector displays the simulator image onto the front of the screen, and the screen absorbs the ball's energy.

**Throw ratio.** A projector specification that describes the relationship between projector distance and image size. Short-throw projectors fit in tight bays; long-throw projectors do not.

**Tee line / hitting position.** The spot the ball is teed up or set down on the mat for each shot.

**Bay alignment.** Whether the hitting position, target line, screen, and launch monitor are all square to each other. Misalignment introduces errors in the data.

## Sim play and practice modes

**Practice / range mode.** Hitting balls at a virtual range with no scoring, often with various target distances marked.

**Sim round / round mode.** Playing a virtual course hole-by-hole, with scoring.

**Closest-to-the-pin (CTP).** A skills game where each shot's distance from a fixed pin is recorded.

**Long drive mode.** A skills game where each shot's total distance is recorded.

**Putting mode.** Either a separate putting feature in the sim software, or skipping putts in favor of a generous gimme distance.

## Swing video and analysis

**Frame rate (FPS).** How many frames per second a camera records. Higher frame rates capture more detail when slowed down.

**Shutter speed.** How long each frame is exposed for. Faster shutter speeds reduce motion blur on the club at impact.

**Down-the-line (DTL).** A swing video angle where the camera is behind the ball, on the target line, looking down the line of play.

**Face-on.** A swing video angle where the camera is perpendicular to the target line, looking at the front of the player.

**Slow motion playback.** Replaying a recorded clip at a fraction of the original speed. The clip itself is not changed; the playback is just slowed down.

**Frame-by-frame scrubbing.** Stepping through a clip one frame at a time, forward or back.

**Side-by-side comparison.** Two clips played at the same time, often in synchronization, so the swings can be compared frame by frame.

## Shot data and review

**Session.** A continuous block of shots, usually defined by a date or a single bay visit.

**Shot tag.** A label attached to a shot to mark something about it (which ball, which club, range vs course, anything).

**Dispersion.** The spread of where shots landed, usually visualized as a scatter plot.

**Standard swing.** A reference swing for a club, saved so future swings with that club can be compared against it.

**Excluded shot.** A shot the user has flagged to leave out of analytics, such as a partial swing, a wrong-club hit, or an obvious mishit.
